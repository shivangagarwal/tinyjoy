### 2. Hacker News — Show HN

Post on a weekday morning (8–10am PT). HN readers value no-BS, real maker energy.

**Title:** `Show HN: TinyJoy – 19 free browser games, no download or account required`

**Opening comment (post immediately after the submission goes live):**
```
Hey HN — maker here.

I built TinyJoy (https://tinyjoy.app) because I kept running into the same problem: I wanted a quick game during a 2-minute break and every option required an app install, an account, or bombarded me with ads before I could start.

The design constraint was strict: zero friction. Open the URL, pick a game, playing within 10 seconds. That's it.

Currently 19 games across 5 categories — Quick Hits (60-second games like Color Match, Number Rush, Reaction Time), Word Games (Word Guess which is Wordle-style with no daily limit, Hangman, Word Scramble), Arcade (Snake, Flappy Jump, Brick Breaker), Puzzle (Sudoku with 4 difficulty levels, Minesweeper, 2048), and Classics (Tic-Tac-Toe, Connect Four, Solitaire).

Built with Next.js, deployed on Vercel. The stack is boring by design — fast load times matter more than clever tech for this use case.

Happy to answer questions about any of the game implementations or the design decisions. And genuinely curious: do HN readers still play browser games for breaks, or has that habit died?
```
